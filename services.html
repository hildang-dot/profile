DIY Car Troubleshooting Guides

FLAT TIRE(How to change a flat tire)

Symptoms: Car pulling, thumping sound, visible deflation.

Tools Needed: Spare tire, jack, lug wrench, owner's manual.

  1. SAFETY FIRST: Pull over safely, turn on hazard lights, engage parking brake, ensure car is on level ground.
  2. Locate your spare tire and tools.
  3. Loosen lug nuts (slightly) on the flat tire while the car is on the ground.
  4. Refer to your car's manual for exact jack points. Place the jack under the frame and raise the car until the tire is off the ground.
  5. Fully remove lug nuts and carefully take off the flat tire.
  6. Mount the spare tire and hand-tighten the lug nuts.
  7. Lower the car until the tire touches the ground, then fully tighten the lug nuts in a star pattern.
  8. Lower the car completely. Store the flat tire and tools.

When to Call a Mechanic: No spare, lug nuts stuck, unsafe location, don't feel confident performing the task.

LOW TIRE PRESSURE LIGHTS ON

Disclaimer: These guides are for minor issues only. Always prioritize safety. If you are unsure or uncomfortable, please contact a professional mechanic via our "Find a Mechanic" page.

Symptoms: TPMS (Tire Pressure Monitoring System) light illuminated on dashboard.

Tools Needed: Tire pressure gauge, access to air pump (gas station).

  1. Find your car's recommended tire pressure (usually on a sticker inside the driver's door jamb or in the manual).
  2. Use a tire pressure gauge to check the pressure in all four tires.
  3. Add air to the tires until they reach the recommended PSI. Do not overinflate.
  4. Drive for a few minutes; the light should turn off.

When to Call a Mechanic: Light stays on after inflation, frequent low pressure in the same tire (could indicate a slow leak), visual puncture in the tire.

BATTERY AND STARTING ISSUES

Symptoms: Engine won't crank, clicking sound when trying to start, dim lights, no power to accessories.

Tools Needed: Jumper cables, another working vehicle.

  1. SAFETY FIRST: Ensure both vehicles are off. Position the working car close but not touching the dead car.
  2. Connect the red clamp of the jumper cable to the positive (+) terminal of the dead battery.
  3. Connect the other red clamp to the positive (+) terminal of the working battery.
  4. Connect the black clamp to the negative (-) terminal of the working battery.
  5. Connect the other black clamp to an unpainted metal surface on the dead car's engine block, away from the battery and moving parts.
  6. Start the working car and let it run for 5-10 minutes.
  7. Try to start the dead car. If it starts, disconnect cables in reverse order (black from dead car, black from working car, red from working car, red from dead car).

When to Call a Mechanic: Car doesn't start after jumping, repeated dead battery, corroded battery terminals, no jumper cables or second vehicle available.
